“For many, many years my family pushed me and nagged me to get to the VA and apply for my benefits. I heard lots of horror stories about the hoops they throw at you from other vets so I wasn’t interested in messing with any of it. Mr. Ryan struck up a conversation with me in the grocery store by asking about my USMC tattoo on my arm. After a few minutes we had hit it off, one vet to another. What came after was an offer of a hand in tackling the VA together. He became a strong wind at my back and sometimes a boot in my backside when I needed it to keep the ball rolling. 13 months after that first conversation and now I have a rating for the broken body I got serving, compensation to cover it and VA healthcare that means I don’t have to spend my money to fix what the Corp broke.”

“Life has been extremely challenging and I’m not going to lie, it’s been a struggle. I’m so Thankful for meeting you and you providing me the support I needed to go through the process to apply for benefits. I’ve now been out of the military longer than I served (14 yrs). My service to my country took a serious toll on my body due to the nature of my service. I signed up to be a Paratrooper and I served proudly in the 82nd Airborne Division and the 101st Airborne Air Assault Division, amongst other High-Speed units. My service to our country is one of my proudest achievements, but our sacrifice is not just our time, it’s also our body, and for some our life. I had been struggling trying to maintain success in the corporate world, in my relationship and in life, all the while telling myself that I would get to submitting my claims to the VA. Days, months, and years flew by and I still hadn’t moved forward with my claim. I now know why. I lacked the mental, and emotional support I needed. As soldiers it was frowned upon to show signs of weakness and possibly be considered a malingerer if you had physical issues that may take time to heal properly. That mentality stays with you along with the shame I felt for needing help. That’s when I met Ryan, while looking for glasses. I commented on his service dog, and we quickly struck up a conversation once he realized I was a Veteran as well. This man stopped what he was doing for himself after asking me if I had filed a claim with the VA, and I said I hadn’t. He instantly understood why I hadn’t done so sooner and offered me his experience and support to ensure I got what I deserved for my service. Ryan, provided me support and guidance through the whole process, and never left his Battle Buddy behind even when I wanted to quit. His understanding of my emotional state allowed him to connect with me in a way that took away the shame and provided me with the strength I needed to go through the process. I know without a doubt, that if it wasn’t for Ryan providing me the support, kindness, and compassion I needed; I would still be without the benefits I now receive. Thank You Ryan for being there for me when I needed you the most!”
Ty R.
U.S. Army Veteran · Dallas, TX

Our Story

Built by a veteran, for veterans

The Veteran Promise Project began with a simple belief: every veteran who needs a cane should carry one that honors their service — not a generic aluminum pole from a medical supply catalog.

Shanon Ryan handcrafts each cane from premium hardwoods, adding custom details like brass fittings, laser engravings, and finishes chosen by the veteran. Every cane is built at no cost to the recipient.

Beyond the workshop, the project helps veterans navigate the VA benefits system — walking alongside them through claims, appeals, and the maze of paperwork that too many veterans face alone.

Every veteran deserves to walk with dignity

Whether you need a cane, want to help, or know a veteran who could use support — we’re here.
