VA Resources

Disability Benefits Questionnaires and helpful links to support your VA claims process.

Disability Benefits Questionnaires (DBQs) are standardized VA forms that your healthcare provider fills out to document the diagnosis, severity, and service connection of a specific condition. A properly completed DBQ gives VA raters exactly what they need to assign a disability rating — and can speed up or even replace a Compensation & Pension (C&P) exam.

Below are the most commonly used DBQs, organized by body system. Download the form, bring it to your treating clinician, and submit it with your claim.

Keeping forms current: Each form below links directly to the VA’s official portal so you always get the latest version. If the VA’s site is ever unavailable, we maintain backup copies. If you have trouble accessing any form, contact us and we’ll get it to you.

Head & Ear

Headaches (Including Migraines)
VA Form 21-0960C-8
Documents headache frequency, duration, severity, and prostrating episodes. Migraine headaches are one of the most commonly claimed conditions among veterans.
Download from VA.gov
Ear Conditions (Vestibular & Infectious)
VA Form 21-0960N-2
Covers Meniere’s disease, vertigo, otitis media, and labyrinthitis. Note: the Hearing Loss and Tinnitus DBQ requires a VA-trained examiner and is not publicly available.
Download from VA.gov

Spine & Back

Back (Thoracolumbar Spine) Conditions
VA Form 21-0960M-14
Documents range of motion, pain on movement, incapacitating episodes, neurological abnormalities, and functional impact. Used for low back strain, lumbar disc disease, radiculopathy, and spondylosis.
Download from VA.gov
Neck (Cervical Spine) Conditions
VA Form 21-0960M-13
Same structure as the back DBQ but for cervical spine. Covers cervical radiculopathy, herniated discs, and post-surgical residuals.
Download from VA.gov

Joints & Musculoskeletal

Knee and Lower Leg Conditions
VA Form 21-0960M-9
Range of motion, instability testing, meniscus pathology, and post-surgical residuals (TKR, ACL/MCL/meniscectomy). Bilateral knee claims may trigger the bilateral factor bonus.
Download from VA.gov
Shoulder and Arm Conditions
VA Form 21-0960M-12
Rotator cuff injuries, AC separation, impingement syndrome. Notes dominant vs. non-dominant arm rating distinctions.
Download from VA.gov
Ankle Conditions
VA Form 21-0960M-2
Documents ankylosis, instability, and post-traumatic arthritis. Pair with a foot DBQ when both ankle and foot are involved.
Download from VA.gov
Hip and Thigh Conditions
VA Form 21-0960M-7
Covers labral tears, hip arthroplasty, and femoral nerve impingement.
Download from VA.gov
Wrist Conditions
VA Form 21-0960M-15
Carpal tunnel syndrome, scaphoid non-union, and post-fusion residuals.
Download from VA.gov

Mental Health

Mental Disorders (Other Than PTSD and Eating Disorders)
VA Form 21-0960P-2
Used for depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, adjustment disorder, and schizophrenia. The General Rating Formula for Mental Disorders covers 0% through 100% tiers.
Download from VA.gov
PTSD — Review
VA Form 21-0960P-4
Used for re-evaluation of existing PTSD ratings. Private clinicians with relevant credentials can complete this form.
Download from VA.gov
Note: Initial PTSD evaluations require a VA-network or contracted clinician. Private DBQs are accepted for review evaluations only.

Respiratory

Respiratory Conditions (Asthma, COPD, and Others)
VA Form 21-0960L-1
Covers asthma, COPD, emphysema, chronic bronchitis, restrictive lung disease, and sarcoidosis. Pulmonary function test (PFT) values drive the rating tier.
Download from VA.gov
Sleep Apnea
VA Form 21-0960L-2
Documents diagnosis (sleep study), severity, and treatment requirements (CPAP use). Commonly filed as a secondary claim connected to PTSD, medication-related weight gain, or sinusitis.
Download from VA.gov
Sinusitis, Rhinitis, and Other Nose/Throat Conditions
VA Form 21-0960F-2
Covers chronic sinusitis and rhinitis. PACT Act presumptive applies to chronic forms of these conditions for eligible veterans.
Download from VA.gov

Heart & Cardiovascular

Heart Conditions (Ischemic and Non-Ischemic Heart Disease)
VA Form 21-0960A-1
METs (Metabolic Equivalents) testing values drive the rating tier. Ischemic heart disease is an Agent Orange presumptive condition.
Download from VA.gov
Hypertension
VA Form 21-0960A-3
Documents blood pressure readings, medication regimen, and end-organ involvement. Now a presumptive condition for Agent Orange and PACT Act exposures.
Download from VA.gov

All DBQ Forms

Complete VA DBQ Library
The forms above cover the most commonly claimed conditions. The VA maintains approximately 70 public DBQs covering everything from skin conditions to diabetes to eye disorders. If your condition isn’t listed above, the full alphabetical library is available on the VA’s website.
