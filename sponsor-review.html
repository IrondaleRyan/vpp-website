On a $150 cane, the gap between Square Free and Stripe’s nonprofit rate is $1.65. Across 100 sponsored canes that’s $165 — roughly one more cane’s worth of wood and hardware. Real, but not enormous.

My honest read: if you don’t have the IRS determination letter yet, stay on Square. No discount is available to you anywhere without it, the rates converge, and opening a second processor buys you nothing right now. Ship it, revisit when the letter lands.

If you do have determination, Stripe is worth the 30 minutes — better rate, and an account in VPP’s name reads as an organization rather than a person.

Either way, the page I built doesn’t change. Swapping processors is a one-line edit on my end.
04 — Open questions

Four things only you can answer

Nothing ships until these are settled. The first two are blockers.

1. Do you have the IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter — or just the EIN?

These get confused constantly, and they’re not the same thing. Navy Federal will open a nonprofit business account on an EIN plus state incorporation papers. The IRS determination letter is a separate document, and it’s the one that matters here.

Why it blocks: it decides whether Stripe’s 2.2% rate is even available to you, and whether the website is legally allowed to describe sponsorships as tax-deductible. Right now the page makes no tax claim at all — deliberately. It stays that way until you confirm.

2. What does a cane actually cost you?

Materials, hardware, finish, shipping — the real number for one finished cane out the door. The tiers on this page ($35 / $75 / $150) are placeholders I made up.

Why it blocks: the whole design is calibrated to “$150 = one full cane.” The cane graphic fills to the handle at $150. If the true cost is $90, the page is telling sponsors something untrue. I’m not shipping a number I invented.

3. Which Square plan are you on — Free, Plus, or Premium?

Free is 3.3% + 30¢ online. Plus and Premium drop it to 2.9% + 30¢, but carry a monthly fee I haven’t verified.

Why it matters: 0.4% swing, and it changes whether Square is actually close to Stripe or noticeably behind.

4. Does Sponsor a Cane replace the GoFundMe, or run alongside it?

You said you wanted this to feel like the nonprofit rather than something tied to you personally. The GoFundMe link is literally titled help-shanon-ryan-craft-quality-canes-for-veterans.

My recommendation: replace it. One clear ask, funds landing in VPP’s Navy Federal account, nothing routed through a personal fundraiser. But it’s your organization and your donor base — if the GoFundMe has momentum you don’t want to lose, we can run both for a transition period.
Sequencing. Don’t let me pull the GoFundMe until the new checkout links exist — otherwise the site has no working way to give, for however long the gap lasts. Order is: confirm 501(c)(3) status → create the checkout links → I drop them in and remove the GoFundMe in a single change. One commit, no downtime.
