The sponsor never needs an account. Not Square, not Stripe, not Zeffy. They type in a card and they’re done — same as buying anything online. All three work this way. (PayPal is the one that nudges donors toward creating an account, which is why it’s not on this list.)

Two technical notes. The checkout screen above is a representative mockup, not a screenshot — the real pages differ in styling and wording, but the shape (amount, wallets, card fields, pay) is accurate. And the tier amounts — $35 / $75 / $150 — are placeholders: the cane graphic fills to “a full cane” at $150. Tell Aaron what a finished cane actually costs in materials and shipping and he’ll set the real numbers. Nothing else about the design changes.

The Zeffy catch, stated plainly. Zeffy is free to you because it asks your sponsor for an optional contribution at checkout — and it’s pre-filled, not blank. Zeffy’s own help pages say roughly 60% of donors contribute, averaging about 4%. A critical review claims the default is pre-selected much higher and that donors click past it without noticing. I could not resolve that from published sources and I’m not going to guess at it.

Before committing: run a $5 test donation through any Zeffy form and look at what that field defaults to. Ten minutes, and it’s the only honest way to answer it. It matters here because these are people sponsoring a cane for a veteran — if they feel a platform quietly upsold them, that costs more than 3% ever will.
